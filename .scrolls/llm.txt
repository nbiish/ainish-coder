### Boarding School Era

The U.S. Indian boarding school system (1819-1969) was explicitly designed to:
- "Kill the Indian, save the man" — The stated mission of Captain Richard Pratt
- Forcibly remove children from families and communities
- Criminalize language — Children were beaten, starved, and isolated for speaking Native languages
- Destroy cultural identity — Traditional clothing, hair, names, and ceremonies were forbidden
- Create intergenerational trauma — Effects persist across generations today
